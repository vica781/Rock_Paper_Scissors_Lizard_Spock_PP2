About - Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock


Ones more Welcome to the world of Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock - an expanded, even more fun and strategic version of the traditional hand game that we all know and love.


The Classic Game

Rock, Paper, Scissors (RPS) is a game with a long history, believed to have originated in China during the Han Dynasty (approximately 200 BC). The game made its way to Japan, where it became known as "Jan-ken", and eventually spread to the West. It's a game of chance, psychology, and strategy, where rock crushes scissors, scissors cuts paper, and paper covers rock.



The game was introduced to the public in "The Lizard-Spock Expansion". Rock Paper Scissors Lizard Spock is a game of chance that expands. It is first used to settle a dispute about what to watch on TV between Sheldon and Raj in "The Lizard-Spock Expansion".


It is mentioned again in "The Rothman Disintegration", where Sheldon interact with Barry Kripke over the office of a retiring professor, and they end up deciding who gets the office by playing a game of Rock, Paper, Scissors, Lizard, Spock. Sheldon explains the rules to Barry.


The game was originally created by Sam Kass with Karen Bryla, as "Rock Paper Scissors Spock Lizard". They submitted it to Hasbro who rejected it.


However, in 2005, they granted permission to Bill Prady to use the game on The Big Bang Theory.


The game is a five-weapon expansion on the classic Rock Paper Scissors. It operates on the same basic principle, but includes two additional weapons: the Lizard (formed by the hand as a sock-puppet-like mouth) and Spock (formed by the Star Trek Vulcan salute). This reduces the chances of a round ending in a tie.
